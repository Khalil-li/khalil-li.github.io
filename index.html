Welcome! I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Institute of Engineering Thermophysics, Chinese Academy of Sciences (IET, CAS) in Beijing, working under the supervision of Prof. Qiangqiang Ren. I conduct my research within the Circulating Fluidized Bed Laboratory and the State Key Laboratory of Clean and Efficient Coal Utilization. My work centers on clean and low-carbon coal combustion, the migration and transformation of alkali and alkaline earth metals (AAEMs) in thermal processes, low-carbon utilization of high-carbon energy, and thermal treatment of solid waste. If you are interested in potential collaborations or have questions about my work, please feel free to reach out.

Research Interests

  • Clean & low-carbon coal combustion
  • AAEMs migration & transformation
  • High-carbon energy low-carbon utilization
  • Solid waste thermal treatment

Research

My research aims to enable cleaner utilization of high-carbon fuels through fundamental understanding of ash chemistry, alkali metal behavior, and combustion pollutant control. Below is an overview of the directions I work on — written for a broader scientific audience.

Clean & Low-Carbon Coal Combustion

Coal remains a cornerstone of global energy supply, yet its combustion poses significant environmental challenges. My work addresses this through purification-combustion technology for high-alkali coal — a particularly problematic feedstock. By decoupling the purification (gasification) stage from the combustion stage, we can separately control inorganic mineral evolution, alkali metal release, and nitrogen oxide formation. This integrated approach achieves ultra-low NOx emissions while avoiding the ash-related operational issues (slagging, fouling) that have historically prevented direct combustion of high-alkali coals.

AAEMs Migration & Transformation

Alkali and alkaline earth metals (AAEMs — principally Na, K, Ca, Mg) in coal are the root cause of ash deposition, slagging, and fouling in utility boilers. I study how these elements migrate between the gas and condensed phases during thermal treatment under varying atmospheres (O2, CO2, H2O) and temperatures. The goal is to develop predictive models for AAEM release, capture, and deposition, and to design operating windows where liquid slag tapping simultaneously removes AAEMs — turning a liability into a controllable separation process.

Low-Carbon Utilization of High-Carbon Energy

Achieving carbon neutrality in the power and industrial sectors requires not just fuel switching but smarter utilization of existing carbon-intensive resources. I investigate the nitrogen migration pathways (fuel-N → NH3/HCN → NOx/N2) in staged purification-combustion systems, aiming to maximize the conversion of fuel-bound nitrogen to harmless N2 rather than NOx. This chemistry-first approach to low-NOx combustion is especially relevant for wide-load operation where conventional de-NOx technologies lose effectiveness.

Solid Waste Thermal Treatment

Industrial solid wastes such as coal gasification fine slag and biomass ash represent both an environmental burden and an underutilized resource. I explore high-temperature treatment routes that convert these wastes into value-added materials — for example, foam glass-ceramics from gasification slag. Understanding the relationship between ash composition, melting behavior, and final material properties is central to designing robust valorization pathways.
